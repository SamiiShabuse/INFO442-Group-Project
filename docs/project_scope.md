## 3. Problem Description

### 3.1 What is the problem you are facing?

Many individual investors are struggling with building a portfolio that matches their risk tolerance. Investors often focus only on high-return assets without understanding volatility, drawdowns, diversification, or how assets move together. This can lead to portfolios that are too risky, poorly diversified, or not aligned with the investor’s goals.

Another challenge is that traditional portfolio analysis often only relies on historical averages. However, market risk changes over time, and recent price behavior, trading volume, volatility, and benchmark movement are able to provide signals about near-future risks. If investors only use historical averages, they may miss changing risk conditions.

Our project addresses this problem by building a predictive portfolio optimization and risk analytics dashboard for users. The dashboard will use historical stock and ETF data to engineer market features, predict future asset volatility, and use those predictions as inputs into portfolio optimization. This project will then compare portfolio strategies such as equal-weight portfolios, minimum-volatility portfolios, maximum-Sharpe portfolios, and risk-profile-based portfolios.

---

### 3.2 Who/what is affected by this problem?

This problem affects individual investors, beginner investors, students, and small financial advisory teams who need better tools to understand portfolio risk and return. It also affects anyone trying to make an investment allocation decision without access to professional portfolio analytical tools.

Beginner investors are especially affected because they may not entirely understand that a portfolio with strong historical returns can still have high future risk, volatility, or poor diversification.

---

### 3.3 How many people/organizations/places/etc. and how much are they affected?

Millions of individual investors participate in financial markets through retirement accounts, brokerage accounts, and investment apps. Poor portfolio allocation can lead to unnecessary losses, excessive volatility, and portfolios that do not match the investor’s risk tolerance.

For this project, impact will be measured using both predictive model metrics and portfolio-level metrics. The predictive model will be evaluated using errors such as MAE and RMSE for future volatility prediction. The portfolio strategies will be evaluated using annualized return, volatility, Sharpe ratio, maximum drawdown, cumulative return, and comparison against baselines such as equal-weight portfolios and the S&P 500 benchmark.

---

### 3.4 Why is solving this problem a priority for your organization?

This is a priority because portfolio construction is a real-world financial decision-making problem. A data-driven dashboard can help users understand the tradeoff between risk and return before making investment decisions.

For our team, this project is extremely valuable as it applies the complete data science pipeline through a practical financial problem. The project includes data acquisition, data cleaning, creating data pipelines, feature engineering, predictive modeling, model evaluation, portfolio optimization, visualization, interpretation, and deployment.

### C. In an ideal world, is there additional data you would want to get/gather that would be relevant to this problem?

In the perfect world, this project would have all of these additional resources that would help improve forward-looking risk estimations.

* **Real-time streaming price data:** Yahoo Finance only gives end-of-day historical pricing. Real-time tick data would give live portfolio monitoring, but this usually requires paid APIs such as Bloomberg.
* **Investor sentiment data:** Social media sentiment or news sentiment scores would add a behavioral side to portfolio construction, but pricing and access could be another issue.
* **Implied volatility data:** VIX term structure or individual stock implied volatility would improve forward-looking risk estimates over general historical volatility.

---

### D. What analysis will the existing data not support?

The existing data can support limited predictive modeling, such as predicting future realized volatility using historical market features. However, it will not support guaranteed future return prediction or personalized investment advice.

The analysis will still be limited because `yfinance` mainly provides historical/end-of-day data, not real-time market data. This project will not support live trading or real-time portfolio monitoring. The data also does not include extra individual investor information such as income, tax situation, current holdings, or investment horizon.

Lastly, the predictive model will estimate future volatility, not guarantee future performance. Historical relationships may change, especially during market shocks. Sector classification data may also be static and may not fully capture historical sector reclassifications.

## 9. Field Trial or Randomized Controlled Trial

A field trial could be designed to test whether the predictive dashboard helps improve users’ understanding of portfolio risk, diversification, and risk-return tradeoffs. The population for the trial would be students or beginner investors who have limited experience with portfolio construction.

Participants can be split into two separate groups:

**Baseline Group:** Participants will receive basic information such as historical returns for selected assets and an equal-weight portfolio.

**Treatment Group:** Participants will use the Predictive Portfolio Optimization and Risk Analytics Dashboard, which will include portfolio allocation, risk metrics, Sharpe ratio, maximum drawdown, correlation heatmaps, and backtest results.

The trial could last up to one class session or one week. Before using the material, both groups will complete a short pre-test measuring their understanding of risk, diversification, volatility, and portfolio comparison. After using either the baseline materials or the dashboard, both groups would complete a post-test.

The outcomes measured could include the ability to identify which portfolios have the highest predicted risk, explain diversification, compare risk-adjusted returns, and interpret volatility, Sharpe ratio, and maximum drawdown.

The baseline would be a simple equal-weight portfolio explanation or static table of asset returns. The treatment would be the interactive dashboard. The analysis would compare improvement from the pre-test to the post-test between the two groups. If possible, results would be compared across subgroups such as students with finance experience versus students without finance experience.
