## Course Description

This course is a capstone course that provides an opportunity for students to apply a data science approach to solve domain problems. Students form a team and challenge a real-world project of their choice.

Each team selects a domain and a data set, and then applies a data science approach to actual situations for real-world decision making. Each team is required to come up with a scientific question with a business value, perform an explorative data analysis, develop a data science model, evaluate the results, and communicate the results with an audience.

---

## Rationale

This course introduces students to real-world data science challenges. It emphasizes that data science lies at the intersection of domain knowledge, hacking skills, and analytical techniques.

The course leads students to apply comprehensive data science knowledge and skills to real-world applications.

---

## Learning Outcomes

Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:

- Describe the interdisciplinary nature of data science as a combination of hacking skills, analytical techniques, and domain knowledge.
- Identify appropriate techniques and tools for acquiring, retrieving, analyzing, and making use of data for a particular application domain such as healthcare and finance.
- Explain the challenges of implementing big data analytics solutions for real-world problems in a chosen domain.
- Formulate domain-specific and context-relevant data analytics questions and hypotheses.
- Apply the analytical techniques currently available to a chosen domain to solve domain-specific problems.
- Create visualizations and communicate data analytics results to a large audience and decision makers.

Academic Dishonesty Policy:

To avoid plagiarizing material, observe the following:

- If you work on an assignment with another student or group of students, make sure that your final individual paper is your own work, or the work of your project group for group projects, unless otherwise specified by the professor.
- While you may discuss the assignment with other students, you must express your own ideas in your own way in your paper.
- If you use printed or electronic resources in your papers, attribute the sources you have used.
- Sources can be attributed by quoting or paraphrasing the material and listing the source in an annotated bibliography.
- Use standard notation when citing references.
